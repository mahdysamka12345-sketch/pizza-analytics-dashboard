# Pizza Sales Analytics Dashboard

<img width="1239" height="500" alt="Pizza Sales Dashboard" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/b65da16b-988a-43ab-9aef-f42885d056e1" />

An interactive data analytics dashboard designed to track, visualize, and analyze pizza sales performance. This project transforms raw transactional data into actionable business insights, highlighting operational trends, customer preferences, and revenue drivers.

## 📊 Dashboard Overview

The dashboard is structured into three primary analytical dimensions to provide a comprehensive look at retail performance:

1. **High-Level KPIs & Filters:**
   * **Total Sales:** 8,436 (Revenue generated across the period)
   * **Total Orders:** 499 (Total unique customer transactions)
   * **Total Quantity Sold:** 511 pizzas
   * **Interactive Slicers:** Dynamic filtering by Pizza Category (Chicken, Classic, Supreme, Veggie), Pizza Size (L, M, S, XL), and Unit Price thresholds.

2. **Sales & Product Performance:**
   * **Order by Pizza Category:** Break down showing Classic (30%) and Veggie (27%) leading categories, followed closely by Supreme (24%) and Chicken (19%).
   * **Sales by Pizza Size:** Clear visibility into customer size preferences, with Large (L) dominating at 47% of total sales, followed by Medium (M) at 27% and Small (S) at 22%.
   * **Top 10 Pizzas by Quantity:** A detailed horizontal bar chart tracking exact quantities sold per pizza type, with *The Classic Deluxe Pizza* (30 units) and *The Pepperoni Pizza* (26 units) taking top spots.

3. **Temporal Trends & Customer Behavior:**
   * **Total Orders per Hour Trendline:** Identifies peak operational hours, showing a distinct rush at 12:00 PM (56 orders) and a major evening peak at 5:00 PM (72 orders), invaluable for staff scheduling and prep management.
   * **Most Active Months:** Monthly distribution of customer orders across May, June (Peak with 54 orders), July, August (51 orders), and September.

## 🛠️ Tech Stack & Skills Demonstrated
* **Data Visualization:** Power BI / Excel Dashboard design
* **Data Modeling:** Relationships, calculated measures, and data cleaning
* **UI/UX Design:** Clean grid layout, tailored muted color palette, Arabic/English bilingual filter support, and user-friendly KPI cards.
* **Business Intelligence:** Trend analysis, product performance ranking, and demand forecasting insights.

## 💡 Key Business Insights
* **Peak Hour Optimization:** Inventory and staffing should be heavily scaled up around 12 PM and 4 PM - 6 PM to handle the clear demand spikes.
* **Inventory Focus:** Large size pizzas represent nearly half of all sales (47%). Dough and box manufacturing/ordering should heavily favor the 'L' size format.
* **Menu Engineering:** Classic and Veggie categories drive over 57% of total orders. Promos could be targeted at lower-performing categories (like Chicken) to balance inventory velocity.

## 🚀 How to Use the Dashboard
1. Open the source project file (`.pbix` or `.xlsx`) in its respective software.
2. Use the interactive panel on the right side to filter data by category, size, or price point.
3. The charts and high-level KPIs will automatically recalculate to display metrics specific to your filtered selection.
